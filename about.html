About BlackCat Robotics

Two founders.
One conviction.

Every robot deployed creates a human job. We're building the AI platform that connects both — diagnostics on one end, skilled hands on the other.

How We Started

It started with a broken drone.

In early 2026, we watched a $15,000 agricultural drone sit dead in a warehouse for three weeks — waiting for a technician who could diagnose a firmware fault. The parts were available. The knowledge existed. But the connection between AI diagnostics and human repair didn't.

The Problem
Robots are deploying faster than humans can maintain them
275,000 commercial drones in service by 2030. 5,500+ humanoids already deployed. 750,000+ warehouse robots at Amazon alone. The maintenance market is projected to reach $10.2 billion by 2033 — but the workforce pipeline is 3:1 short on demand.
The Answer
AI diagnostics paired with human technicians
TechMedix AI handles what machines do best — pattern recognition, predictive failure analysis, fleet-wide anomaly detection. Our network of certified human technicians handles what only humans can — physical repair, calibration, and the judgment calls that no algorithm makes alone.
"

The future runs on robots. We keep them running. Not by replacing technicians with algorithms — by giving technicians algorithms that make them superhuman.

— BlackCat Robotics, founding principle
What We Believe

Three things we don't compromise on.

01
Validation before features
We don't build what we think the market wants. We fix real robots, talk to real technicians, and ship what the data shows is broken. Every feature starts with a repair ticket, not a roadmap slide.
02
AI augments, never replaces
TechMedix AI predicts failure 48 hours before it happens. But the technician who shows up, opens the chassis, and replaces the motor — that's a human. We build tools that make skilled trades more powerful, not obsolete.
03
Open by default
Our 185-question certification curriculum is open source. Our diagnostic models improve with every repair logged. The robotics maintenance industry is too new for walled gardens — we share what we learn because a bigger ecosystem lifts everyone.
